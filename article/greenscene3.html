Sustainable Animation:

Transforming Studios for the Environment

Writer: Muthiah Naurah Arafah
Editor: Sitti Afifah S. Halada

Overview

Nowadays, every industry has made the concept of sustainability a crucial focus, including the animation sector. The concept of sustainability is the principle of meeting the needs of today without compromising the right of future generations to meet their own needs. Sustainability is not only good for our planet, but also for business. By implementing green practices, the animation industry can:

  • Reduce their carbon footprint.
  • Improve company image.
  • Attract clients and consumers who care about environmental issues.

    • Michelle Connolly, director at Educational Voice—a studio animation—“Each phase, be it storyboarding or the final render, adds to our (studio) carbon ledger. It’s our duty to minimize these footprints.”

    Understanding the concept of sustainability, the animation industry is starting to transform for the environment. Animation studios can transform by implementing the following steps:

    Using eco-friendly technology Animation studios require significant energy resources in various ways, such as lighting and rendering processes. Energy consumption can be diverted using renewable energy sources in the studio. Also, it is possible to optimize the HVAC system in the studio and upgrade the lighting sources to LED lighting.

    Using eco-friendly hardware and software Nowadays, animation studios opt to use more energy-efficient hardware and software designed to consume less power. This move can help reduce electricity consumption. Besides, animation studios can opt for sustainable cloud rendering providers.

    Using virtual production technology By adopting virtual production technology, animation studios can significantly reduce their carbon footprint. Instead of building physical sets that take up a lot of space and resources, animators can create environments and characters digitally. This allows teams to work collaboratively from anywhere, thus enabling an indirect reduction in carbon footprint.

    Using eco-friendly materials Studios use materials in production to build sets, properties, and visual effects needed to realize a story or concept. The importance of using biodegradable and recyclable materials in animation production to reduce waste and environmental impact, such as utilizing paper and natural fibers in animation production.

    Waste management The use of materials in production can produce waste that, if not managed properly, will have a negative impact on the environment. For this reason, animation studios should establish reduction strategies and recycling protocols to manage waste. In addition, these studios often reuse sets and props to reduce waste and lessen the environmental impact of hand-crafted storytelling methods.

    Following the Environmental Media Association (EMA) guidelines Environmental Media Association (EMA) is a non-profit organization dedicated to inspiring and empowering the media industry to create positive change for the environment. EMA not only provides guidance and resources for adopting green technologies, but also inspires animators to create narratives that bring awareness to the importance of protecting the environment. Through its strong network, EMA facilitates collaboration between animators, environmentalists, and policy makers.

    Just as studios are evolving to embrace sustainable practices, let's be mindful of the impact our choices have!
