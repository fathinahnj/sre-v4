Holiday Season:

Movies and Books about Environment to Spend Your Holiday Season

Written by: Social Media Strategist

It's the Holiday Season! There are many activities you can do during this holiday season. Travelling, going home to meet your family, or just lazing around in your room as long as you want!

But, it's incomplete to spend time during the holidays without watching a film or finishing those books on your reading list. In case you need film or book recommendations, here are 5 recommendations for movies and books with the theme of sustainability and environment! Let's check it out!

Films:

  • Semes7a (2018)

    • A documentary film directed by Chairun Nissa brings up the theme of the harmony between nature and humans. With a duration of 1.5 hours, Semes7a discusses how to see climate change through the perspective of faith and religion. The religion and culture of the Indonesian people are in harmony with how nature works. Through 7 people from 7 different provinces, share their efforts to care for nature based on the teachings of their religion and culture.

  • Our Mother’s Land (2020)

    • With a duration of 55 minutes, this film tells the story of women environmental fighters in Indonesia. Journalist Febriana Firdaus travelled around Indonesia to meet women from indigenous communities who are driving the resistance to defend their land from corporate exploitation. In addition, the film highlights how the struggles of women activists face challenges from their own communities.

  • The Salt of The Earth (2014)

    • In this 1 hour and 50 minute documentary film, Sebastiao Salgado shares his life and work as a photographer documenting societies in hidden corners of the world. The film highlights how these populations face complex issues such as hunger, climate crisis, and other social problems.

  • Tenggelam Dalam Diam (2021)

    • This documentary film produced by Watchdoc and Greenpeace highlights coastal cities on Java island that face the threat of rising sea levels. From here, they connect various climate issues such as the use of unclean energy, pollution, deforestation, to the increase in earth's temperature which causes polar ice to melt which then has an impact on rising sea levels which can then drown a population centre.

  • Extinction: The Facts (2020)

    • Narrated by David Attenborough, this documentary discusses how the earth is entering its sixth man-made extinction. The film discusses how human actions and developments have pushed one million species to the brink of extinction. It also discusses solutions that can slow down and prevent this from happening.

    Books:

  • This Changes Everything: Capitalism vs. The Climate

    • This book by Naomi Clain provides a detailed analysis of how we need to change the economic system to stop global warming. It also explains why large-scale civil resistance is the only way to bring about such change.

  • Cradle to Cradle: Remaking the Way We Make Things

    • Through this book, readers are informed about the concept of waste treatment that goes beyond the 3R approach (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle). In addition, the book written by William McDonough and Michael Braungart further explains the design of waste treatment that aims to eliminate the concept of waste in the first place.

  • Menanam Adalah Melawan by Widodo

    • Written by Widodo, a farmer from Kulonprogo, the book tells the story of farmers' struggle to defend their living space. For years, they fought sand mining companies by planting. The book also explains the condition of Indonesian farmers who are still vulnerable to intimidation and criminalisation when they defend their rights.

  • Plan B 4.0: Mobilisasi untuk Menyelamatkan Peradaban

    • Written by Lester Brown, this book helps readers envision a renewable energy economy with the steps to get there.

  • The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History

    • This book written by Elizabeth Colbert discusses how the earth is facing the sixth extinction. The book tells how humans are witnessing this through various events that are happening today such as: mass ecocide of animals, plants, and insects.
